# WEEK_08

## Part A
* Code Adaptation: I adapted the project framework from the previous week, modifying the logic so that the synthesizer only triggers an audio output exactly on the first beat of each bar, remaining silent during the other beats.
* Gain Staging Issues: While mixing the pre-recorded sampled audio with real-time synthesized audio, I encountered significant relative level balancing issues. The `.wav` recordings played via `SoundFile` are typically pre-mastered and extremely loud, whereas unconstrained pure sine waves generated by `SinOsc` easily induce severe digital clipping.
* Mixdown Calibration: Initially, the synthesizer completely overwhelmed the drum track. To resolve this, I implemented a strict mixdown within the `setup()` function, capping the `drumLoop` `.amp()` at 0.6 and attenuating the maximum volume of the `synthOsc` to 0.3. This allocation reserves 0.1 of headroom for the master bus, ensuring a clean, clear output free of audio distortion or pops.

## Part B
* Introducing Randomness: Pre-recorded audio and video elements are rigid and static. By controlling the triggering of every single beat programmatically through code, I introduced stochastic elements into the performance. This includes a 30% probability of a glass sound effect triggering, dynamically shifting pitch rates, and generating geometric shapes at random coordinates upon every trigger.
* Rhythmic Visuals: Translating musical beats into a jumping geometric language on the screen allows viewers to intuitively "see" the structural layers of the musical rhythm through immediate visual pulsations.

---

## Critical Architectural Flaw
* Physical Bottleneck: This architecture suffers from a highly critical, underlying structural defect: the complete disconnect between audio execution and the rendering frame rate.
* Timing Desynchronization: Musical tempo requires absolute, millisecond-level precision. In Processing, if the `draw()` loop experiences latency or drops frames due to heavy graphical rendering overloads, the growth rate of `frameCount` slows down accordingly. 
* Impact on BPM: This instantly decelerates the calculated BPM, completely throwing off the timing of the entire musical rhythm.
